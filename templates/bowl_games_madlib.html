An Adverb tells how something is done. It modifys a verb and usually ends in ly. Modestly, stupidly, and carefully are adverb.

A Noun is the name of a person, place, or thing. Sidewalks, umbrella, bridle, bathtub, and nose are noun.

A Verb is an action word. Run, pitch, jump and swim are verbs. Put verbs in past tense if the direction says PAST TENSE. Ran, pitched, jumped, and swam are verbs in past tense.
